Prove The Value Before The Land Fight

If the first question is "which block?", the idea gets dragged into local gravity too early. Start with the ledger. What must any suitable inland space do for Straddie before it earns the right to be considered: sleep, simulate, compute, train, care and stay useful in a crisis?

Capsule nights3k-12k

Annual planning range for a small to medium concept, depending on capsule count and occupancy.

Simulation hours8k-30k+

More than two civic simulation or training hours for every occupied capsule night, before extra workshops.

Idle GPU-hours40k-250k+

What A Real Space Must Carry

A good site is not just available land. It is a load-bearing civic instrument. These are the loads it should be able to carry.

Local beds

Visitor overflow, event staff, visiting health workers, short placements and quiet rest blocks.

AI literacy

Hands-on civic AI, clean-city mapping, local dashboards, prompt practice and agent-readable exports.

Health surge

Rest, intake support, telehealth logistics, visiting specialists, wellbeing education and private records.

Community funding

Member use, public access blocks, support packages, workshops, retreats, events and sponsor-backed sessions.

Emergency flexibility

Temporary beds, local communications, offline files, staff support and staged response drills.

Idle compute

Capsule GPUs for disaster kiosks, noticeboard builds, Virtual Minjerribah tests and local AI training when capsules are not occupied.

Rack pathway

A measured route toward a serious local supercompute rack, with power, cooling, operators, data governance and real workloads proven first.

Planning evidence

Source-dated metrics that can help grants, supporters, government and community reviewers make calmer decisions.
