The Thesis

Islands do not fail because they lack imagination. They fail because every useful spare room is already full when pressure arrives. A capsule hotel can be more than cheap sleep: it can be the island's rehearsal room for visitors, staff, care, food, waste, transport, AI, compute and disaster response.

This is readiness with a bed.

The public case is not that this belongs on one secretly chosen block. The public case is that Straddie deserves a way to compare inland spaces by what they can carry: beds, learning, local jobs, emergency flexibility, health-support logistics and civic AI practice.

Capsules

Beds that can switch from visitor use to workforce rest, visiting specialists, event staff or emergency accommodation blocks.

Capsule GPUs

Graphics processors that serve occupants first, then join the island compute pool for local models, notices, maps and simulation batches.

Simulation rooms

Rooms where maps, AI agents and locals pressure-test public works, waste, food logistics, health support and civic-twin scenarios.

NFP loop

A community infrastructure engine that can return value as local employment, access blocks, training seats, housing-pressure relief and island capacity.
